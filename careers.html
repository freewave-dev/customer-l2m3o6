Careers

Engineering for a network that matters.

Medical ANS engineers build networks that hospitals, cancer centers, and ambulatory clinics depend on. The work is technical, the stakes are real, and the customer is always a clinical team.

Why here

What we offer.

Healthcare networking is a hard, important, under-served domain. Our engineers spend their time on real architectural problems, not chasing tickets.

  • Named-principal model — engineers own engagements end-to-end
  • Hardware-agnostic stack. We design with what fits, not what we sell
  • Quarterly business reviews where engineers present the technical wins to client executives
  • Direct carrier engineering relationships, including the T-Mobile healthcare practice
  • Remote-first with quarterly engineering off-sites
  • Generous health benefits and learning budget
